Game Review Project

For this project, I decided to analyze the 1994 Sonic the Hedgehog title "Sonic & Knuckles" by playing the game on an online SEGA Genesis emulator on the emulation website PlayClassic.games and analyzing what goes on in the game as I played. I wanted to play "Sonic & Knuckles" because I am a big Sonic the Hedgehog fan, and I wanted to find a popular classic game in the franchise that I have not tried out.

Gameplay

As Sonic the Hedgehog

After the special stage ended, I was taken back to the place in Mushroom Hill Zone where I initially found the ring that enabled me to enter the special stage. The ring is not there this time, which means the special stage appears once.

As Knuckles the Echidna

After getting stuck on Mushroom Hill Zone Act 2 as Sonic, I decided to begin Knuckles the Echidna's campaign in the game. Knuckles's campaign is played like Sonic's campaign, but with some new controls such as gliding after jumping, climbing on walls, and breaking through rocks while running.

Unlike Sonic's campaign, there are some differences in the story, primarily cutscene placement and what the cutscenes depict, and how bosses interact with Knuckles.

Progressing through Act 2 as Knuckles is easier than as Sonic because I can easily crash through rock walls (especially where I got stuck when playing as Sonic!) and glide through the air. This feature makes me believe it would be easier for me to play the game as Knuckles because of these benefits.

Overall Analysis

The features that help me create an illusion of exploring a world include paths for me to travel on and vibrant scenery like a forest-like atmosphere with plenty of mushrooms for Mushroom Hill Zone. Despite being 16-bit, the graphics effectively illustrate fantastical landscapes with cartoony-looking characters that make the game light-hearted.

The fantastical settings are vibrant and mostly well-made which makes it easy for me to navigate. Some parts of the game are hard to impossible for me to progress no matter what I do to solve the problem. The two acts I saw are distinguished by different changes in color in both the foreground and background, along with changes in background music that make them different from each other.

This game allows me to choose between two characters: Sonic the Hedgehog and Knuckles the Echidna. Sonic the Hedgehog is the main protagonist, and his controls involve running, jumping, and spin-dashing through stages. Knuckles the Echidna is one of Sonic’s obstacles in the game, and when playing as Knuckles, his controls are almost the same as Sonic’s. What makes Knuckles different from Sonic is that Knuckles can glide after jumping, climb on walls, and even smash through rocks while running.

The interactions I have with NPCs mainly involve attacking them if they are robotic enemies that hinder my progress. Some NPCs are small animals that are trapped inside the robots that I must rescue by jumping on the robots.

This game involves Sonic the Hedgehog or Knuckles the Echidna saving their world from the evil Dr. Eggman. I get stuck in stages where I cannot find a way to progress even if I try, such as a very tall ramp with a blocked corridor in it. I make progress by completing acts and defeating bosses in the game. In this game, I collect rings to score points and protect my health, jump on springs, perform acrobatic stunts on bars, and jump on robots to rescue small animals trapped inside them. In the special stage, I run through blue spheres to turn them into rings for me to collect.

The music in the Mushroom Hill Zone stages sounds funky, adding to the 90s vibe of the game. One act sounds moderately fast and funky, while the other act sounds a little faster and funkier, making it different. In the special stage, the music sounds upbeat and joyful, adding to the excitement of earning more points in the special stage. In the boss battles, the music sounds energetic, pumping me up for the battle ahead. The sound effects in the game sound cartoony, such as slide whistle-like noises when jumping, sparkly noises when collecting or dropping rings, and whirring noises when spin-dashing.

The game does not comment on itself in a “meta” way. It also does not show any cultural issues from our world that I saw reflected in this game.

Some improvements that this game could include are adding save points for me to progress from where I left off instead of starting all the way back from the beginning when losing all lives and adding hints on how I can progress through the stages if I get stuck.
